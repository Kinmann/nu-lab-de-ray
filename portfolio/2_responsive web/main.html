CHARLOTTE SMITHERS

Charlotte started her dance journey at an early age, and her love of movement led her to explore Ballet, Jazz, Hip Hop, Belly Dancing, Burlesque, Aerial Hoop and Silks in addition to partner dancing. Encouraged by her mother’s love of Ballroom dancing, Charlotte began Ballroom and Latin dancing in 2002 and joined the Go Dance instructor team in 2014. She loves the sassy energy of Samba, the smooth flow and elegant progression of Foxtrot, and being able to dance West Coast Swing to her latest favorite songs. She has experience competing on the Country Western and Ballroom circuits.

HIPHOP DANCE

Hip-hop dance is a range of street dance styles primarily performed to hip-hop music or that have evolved as part of hip-hop culture.

URBAN DANCE

The Urban Dance style stems mainly from hip-hop and street dance styles (house, funk, pop, lock, and breaking) and also draws inspiration from other dance styles, such as: jazz, contemporary, tap, and more.

B-BOY

Breaking, also called breakdancing or b-boying/b-girling, is an athletic style of street dance originating from the African American community in the United States.

CHOREOGRAPHY

Choreography is the art or practice of designing sequences of movements of physical bodies (or their depictions) in which motion or form or both are specified.

JAZZ FUNK

Jazz funk is a lyrical style of dance, perfect for storytelling and projecting emotional depth. It’s exciting and suggestive but without the kind of vulgarity that would undermine its mainstream appeal.

BALLET

Ballet (French: [balɛ]) is a type of performance dance that originated during the Italian Renaissance in the fifteenth century and later developed into a concert dance form in France and Russia.
